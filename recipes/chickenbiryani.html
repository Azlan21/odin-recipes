Chicken Biryani Recipe

This is an Odin recipe: structured, fundamental, and repeatable. No shortcuts that skip technique, no restaurant tricks—just the core process behind real Pakistani chicken biryani. Fragrant rice, deeply spiced chicken, and clean layering. Master this once, and you can scale or tweak it forever.

Ingredients:

Chicken:

  • 1 kg chicken (bone-in preferred)
  • ¾ cup plain yogurt
  • 2 medium onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 3 tbsp oil or ghee

Spices:

  • 1½ tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1½ tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1½ tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder

Rice:

  1. 2 cups basmati rice
  2. Water (for boiling)
  3. 1½ tsp salt
  4. 2-3 whole cloves
  5. 2-3 green cardamom pods
  6. 1 small bay leaf

Layering & Finish

  • ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • ½ cup fresh mint
  • 2 tbsp milk (optional: with a pinch of food color or saffron)
  • Fried onions (optional garnish)

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice 30 minutes. Boil water with salt and whole spices. Add rice and cook until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pot. Fry onions until deep golden. Remove half for layering.
  3. In the same pot, add ginger-garlic paste. Cook briefly, then add tomatoes and all ground spices. Cook until oil separates.
  4. Add chicken and yogurt. Cook covered on medium heat 15-20 minutes until chicken is tender and masala thick.
  5. On top of chicken, add half the rice, then fried onions, mint, cilantro. Add remaining rice. Drizzle milk on top.
  6. Cover tightly. Cook on very low heat 15-20 minutes.
  7. Turn off heat. Rest 5 minutes. Gently mix before serving.
